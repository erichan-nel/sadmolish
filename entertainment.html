Music

Depression is among the ten most frequent indicators for using alternative therapies, and music is one of the remedies for this condition (Astin, 1998). In mental health services, music therapy is regularly offered through group and individual programs following a process of referral and assessment. Music has been found to increase circulating endorphins (Mockel et al., 1994), which are associated with mood changes. In this way a music intervention is expected to improve depression.
