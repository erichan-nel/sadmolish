2.3.1 Persistent Depressive Disorder

Persistent depressive disorder (PDD) is the mood experience of depression that people faced for at least two years. It is common depression that people faced but it is categorized as chronic depression. The way to detect it is PDD is when people have poor appetite or overeating, insomnia lack of sleep, always feel tired and have low self-esteem (Keller et al. 1995).


2.3.2 Postpartum Depression

This type of depression much more serious than the mild depressive and anxiety symptoms. It is related to the social, chemical, and emotional changes associated with having a baby. This depression faced by many new mothers experience. However, postpartum can be treated by medication counselling. Hormones and chemical changes will rapid drop after delivery a baby.


2.3.3 Psychotic Depression

The person who faced psychotic depression (PD) may hear voice or seeing upsetting things that normal people cannot see or hear (hallucinations). They might think others can see or hear same with them but in fact it is not happen. They might think they faced guilt like having crime and wanted by the police but they did not commit any crime. People who having psychotic depression spend a lot of time themselves with bed and stay awake at night.


2.3.4 Seasonal Affective Disorder

Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) is the depression that related to the seasons changes especially in the four seasons country. Usually it starts and end in the same times on every seasons. Most commonly the symptoms are in winter. People tend to sleep too much and lack of energy in daily life. The energy drag to moody feel by the person. Patient with seasonal affective disorder hard to balancing the neurotransmitter serotonin that act for balancing mood (McMahon et al., 2014).


2.3.5 Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder (BD) is associated with mortality risk with patients attempting suicide is 25 percent and patient that had commit suicide was 11 percent. Improper care, mental health solutions are the main causes of the BD. In general, BD patient experiences episodes of irritable mood called mania or also known as hypomania. The existed study has show that the prevalence of 3.7 percent been revealed by using Mood Disorder Questionnaire.
